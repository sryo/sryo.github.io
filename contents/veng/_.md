## Overview

I designed for the researchers, agronomists, and analysts who needed SAOCOM satellite imagery but had to go through agencies and paperwork to get it. SAOCOM is an Argentine constellation that captures Earth imagery around the clock in any weather, and most of the people who could use it never saw it.

We built an e-commerce platform to change that. Users browse a map, filter by date and resolution, preview images, and pay online. For areas not in the catalog, they submit a tasking request for new captures.

## What we shipped

This was a working product, not a prototype. Users could:

- Search an archive by location, date, and cloud cover
- Preview low-resolution imagery before purchase
- Buy and download high-resolution files
- Request new satellite passes for custom areas

I worked with the VENG team to understand the satellites' technical constraints and translated those into interface decisions. SAR imagery is unfamiliar to most users, so the design leaned on familiar e-commerce patterns: search, preview, add to cart, checkout.

## Results

METRICS:
- **24h** catalog order delivery
- **4 global partners** (Ursa Space, e-GEOS, Telespazio, Suhora)
- **Catalog + tasking** dual capture flow
